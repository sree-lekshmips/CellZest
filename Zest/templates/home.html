Nutrition Plans

For anemia, focus on iron-rich foods like red meat, legumes, and leafy greens. Include Vitamin C-rich foods like citrus fruits to improve iron absorption. Add folate-rich foods like spinach and lentils, and ensure enough Vitamin B12 from meat, fish, and eggs. A balanced diet with these foods can help manage anemia, but it’s important to consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.

Exercise Program

Exercise can help boost energy and circulation in people with anemia, but it’s important to start slow. Activities like walking, light yoga, and gentle cycling are good options. Light strength training can also build endurance over time. Always listen to your body and consult a doctor before starting any new exercise, especially if symptoms are severe.

Diet Program

A diet for anemia includes iron-rich foods like meat, leafy greens, beans, and fortified cereals. Vitamin C-rich foods (citrus fruits, peppers) help absorb iron, while B12 (meat, dairy) and folate (greens, beans) support red blood cell production. Avoid coffee, tea, and high-calcium foods around iron-rich meals, as they can reduce absorption.
